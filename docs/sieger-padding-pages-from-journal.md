# Sieger Padding Pages From Journal

These are candidate text-heavy pages for padding the square paperback from `22` to `24` pages while staying close to the journal.

Primary source:

- `/Users/sky/Downloads/Journal2-2.doc`

Most relevant entries:

- `3 February 1900` for the Arnhem trip
- `2 March 1900` for the first Leek visit
- `8 March 1900` for the letter and poster to Leek
- `11 March 1900` for the return ride and meeting

## Recommended Approach

Add two text-heavy opening pages before the existing story pages.

Why this works:

- it keeps the current bike ending as the real ending
- it makes the extra pages feel documentary rather than padded
- it lets the book announce itself as journal-based in Sieger's own voice

## Recommended Draft

### Added Page A

From 1 January till 3 February I had not kept a journal,
and I had already forgotten much.

So I tried to recall everything
as accurately as possible.

There was much sickness in Holland that winter.
Influenza was severe in many places.

Then word came from Arnhem
that Brother Lau Keilholz had died,
and help was needed there.

### Added Page B

A month later, on 2 March,
I rode by bicycle to Leek,
about three and a half hours from Groningen,
to visit a family there.

In the house was Sister Drent's mother,
about eighty-nine years old and very sick.

They all wanted to hear about the gospel.

Later I wrote to Leek again
and arranged a meeting there
at Widow Drent's house.

## Closer-To-Journal Version

If you want the added pages to sound more like direct diary entries, this is the tighter source-close version.

### Added Page A

From 1 January till now I haven't kept a journal,
and I have forgotten a lot, therefore.

So I will try to recall everything
as accurately as possible.

Then we got word of the death
of Brother Lau Keilholz from Arnhem,
and were asked to help there.

### Added Page B

At ten that morning I went on a trip to Leek by bike,
three and a half hours from here,
to visit a family.

Her mother was very sick,
a woman of about eighty-nine.

They all wanted to hear about the gospel.

I later wrote to Leek
and a poster was to be pasted up there,
so we could hold a meeting
at Widow Drent's house.

## Other Good Lines We Could Still Use

- "I was very tired because I had had the wind against me."
- "The first time in my life that that happened."  This refers to helping dress Brother Lau's body and place it in the casket.
- "The bed where the patient was lying was terribly exposed."
- "We had a pleasant time and a beautiful meeting."

## My Recommendation

Use the `Recommended Draft` section rather than the `Closer-To-Journal Version`.

It stays faithful to the journal, reads more smoothly in a children's book, and gives us two substantial pages without sounding like filler.
