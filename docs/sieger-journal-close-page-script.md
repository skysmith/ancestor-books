# Sieger Journal-Close Page Script

This is the plainer, closer-to-journal page script for the square Sieger book.

Primary source notes:

- Arnhem story: `Journal2-2.doc`, entry dated `3 February 1900`
- Leek story: `Journal2-2.doc`, entries dated `2 March 1900`, `8 March 1900`, and `11 March 1900`

This script is meant to match the current `24`-page square layout:

- page `1`: title page
- page `2`: source note
- pages `3-14`: Arnhem story
- pages `15-24`: Leek story

## Page Text

### Page 2

Retold from Sieger Springer's journal entries for 3 February and 2-11 March 1900.

Story one follows his trip to Arnhem after the death of Brother Lau Keilholz.

Story two follows his ride to Leek, where he found a sick woman in a cold bed and helped cover the roof with old sacks.

### Page 3

In the winter of 1900,
word came from Arnhem.

Brother Lau Keilholz had died.

Sieger was asked to go help.

### Page 4

He rode first by bicycle
from Groningen to Meppel.

From Meppel
he took the train to Zwolle.

### Page 5

The wind was against him.

By the time he reached Zwolle,
he was very tired.

### Page 6

In Zwolle he did not know
any names.

So he kept asking questions.

### Page 7

By asking more and more people,
he found Brother and Sister Tassche.

He slept there that Saturday night.

### Page 8

On Sunday the weather was bad.
It snowed.

He could not go on by bicycle.

### Page 9

So he took the train to Arnhem.

On the train he met
Brother van Dam.

### Page 10

In Arnhem everything was all right.

They were still able
to hold a meeting.

### Page 11

The Saints treated him kindly.

Brother Lau was not buried there.

### Page 12

Sieger helped dress the body
and later place it in the casket.

It was the first time in his life
he had done that.

### Page 13

He also visited Brother Emont
in the hospital.

After some days,
he started home.

### Page 14

He went back to Zwolle by train,
picked up his bicycle,
and rode into the wind again
until he reached Assen.

From there he went to Groningen.

### Page 15

On Friday, March 2,
Sieger rode by bicycle
to Leek.

A family there wanted
to hear about the gospel.

### Page 16

The ride from Groningen
took about three and a half hours.

### Page 17

In the house was Sister Drent's mother.

She was about eighty-nine years old
and very sick in bed.

### Page 18

The bed where she lay
was badly exposed to the cold.

### Page 19

So they went outside
to do something about it.

### Page 20

They put something on the roof
for protection.

### Page 21

They used old sacks.

It was plain work,
but it helped.

### Page 22

The bed was less exposed
to the cold than before.

### Page 23

Later Sieger wrote to Leek
and arranged a meeting
at Widow Drent's house.

### Page 24

On Sunday, March 11,
he and Brother van der Werff
rode there again,
held the meeting,
and came home that evening.
