# Illustration Brief

## Project

**Title:** Captain Daniel and the Tory's Honey  
**Format:** 32-page picture book dummy  
**Audience:** Picture-book readers; suspenseful, playful, and historically grounded  
**Story function:** A daylight suspense story about hidden honey, quick lies, and relief through laughter

## Visual Goal

Make this book feel tighter, more intimate, and more verbally driven than Book 4. The engine here is not motion across space. It is the pressure of being watched while everyone tries to keep a secret.

The tone should balance tension and wit without turning broad or goofy. The humor comes from human quick thinking under pressure.

## Overall Art Direction

- Use painterly historical realism with clear staging and expressive faces.
- Keep the camp environment grounded and readable.
- Let props matter: chest, cask, handkerchief, honey, swollen mouth.
- Build suspense through eye-lines, posture, and where the Tory is looking.
- Use composition to make readers feel how close discovery is.

## Tone And Pacing

- Early spreads: immediate problem and controlled courtesy
- Middle spreads: narrowing search and rising panic
- Late spreads: clever improvisation, scrutiny, then release
- Final spreads: laughter and lightness after danger

## Character Notes

### Daniel Cook

- Young, quick, and steady
- In this book he is less the physical action center and more part of the quick-thinking response
- His fast answer should feel natural, not theatrical

### The Captain

- Calm authority
- Knows how to manage the Tory without visible panic
- His composure should help hold the group together

### The Tory

- Suspicious, intent, and nosy
- Moves visually through the camp like a threat
- Should feel smart enough that the trick might fail

### Bee-Stung Soldier

- Important comic-tension figure
- Must not become slapstick
- Mouth and expression should be readable but not grotesque

## Setting Notes

- Morning army camp
- Tents, chests, mess area, and pathways should create visual opportunities for searching
- The chest and cask must be staged clearly
- This book benefits from a more confined visual world than the night stories

## Palette And Light

- Dominant palette: pale morning gray-blue, canvas beige, weathered wood, muted earth
- Accent colors: honey gold, small warm notes in camp gear or interior wood, subtle red-brown in clothing or blankets
- The honey should be one of the warmest colors in the book

## Recurring Visual Motifs

- Eyes watching
- Hands opening, pointing, wiping, hiding
- Honey glow against dull camp colors
- Mouths: speaking, holding back, swollen, nearly laughing

## Continuity Priorities

- Daniel should remain visually consistent with earlier books
- The captain should be clearly distinguishable from Daniel
- The Tory's path through camp should stay spatially clear
- The chest and cask must remain consistent once introduced

## Front Matter Suggestions

### Page 1: Half-Title

- Spot image of honey dripping from a spoon, cask edge, or a closed chest latch

### Page 2: Copyright

- Minimal spot art or open page

### Page 3: Title Page

- Morning camp with the Tory entering

### Page 4: Opening Illustration / Silent Lead-In

- A chest in camp or a wary glance before the story begins

## Spread-By-Spread Art Notes

### Spread 1 (Pages 5-6)

**Text beat:** The honey was taken, but the story continues when the Tory arrives at camp.

**Visual objective:** Begin with immediate tension.

**Composition ideas:**

- Tory entering camp while soldiers register concern
- Morning light, but emotionally tight

**Important details:**

- Make it clear this is a continuation of an earlier event without needing prior knowledge

### Spread 2 (Pages 7-8)

**Text beat:** The captain calmly offers to search.

**Visual objective:** Set up the game of nerves.

**Composition ideas:**

- Captain centered and composed
- Tory leaning in, unconvinced

**Important details:**

- Calm language should contrast with rising inner tension

### Spread 3 (Pages 9-10)

**Text beat:** The Tory searches through the camp.

**Visual objective:** Show the danger moving closer.

**Composition ideas:**

- Walking search through tents and boxes
- Use repeated motions of peering, lifting, checking

**Important details:**

- The camp should feel full of possible hiding places

### Spread 4 (Pages 11-12)

**Text beat:** The search narrows to the chest and the cask.

**Visual objective:** Tighten suspense.

**Composition ideas:**

- Strong focus on the chest
- Tory's body angle or gaze drawing the eye toward it

**Important details:**

- The cask should look ordinary enough to help the trick work

### Spread 5 (Pages 13-14)

**Text beat:** A soldier pulls out a honey-covered hand.

**Visual objective:** Show near-disaster.

**Composition ideas:**

- Tight view of hand, honey, and lid
- Faces in the background registering alarm

**Important details:**

- Honey should be visually rich and unmistakable

### Spread 6 (Pages 15-16)

**Text beat:** The lid slams and the soap line lands.

**Visual objective:** Turn panic into quick improvisation.

**Composition ideas:**

- Handkerchief, honey hand, and spoken line in one scene
- Let the reader see how absurd and plausible the moment feels at once

**Important details:**

- This is the story's signature image beat

### Spread 7 (Pages 17-18)

**Text beat:** Daniel answers quickly and the Tory studies them.

**Visual objective:** Hold the lie under pressure.

**Composition ideas:**

- Daniel and Tory facing each other across small space
- Others trying not to react

**Important details:**

- Daniel's answer should feel fast and believable

### Spread 8 (Pages 19-20)

**Text beat:** The Tory notices the bee-stung soldier.

**Visual objective:** Introduce a second threat before the first resolves.

**Composition ideas:**

- Tory's attention suddenly redirected
- Bee-stung soldier slightly separated

**Important details:**

- The swelling should be clear but child-appropriate

### Spread 9 (Pages 21-22)

**Text beat:** "What ails that man?" / "He has the toothache."

**Visual objective:** Peak verbal suspense.

**Composition ideas:**

- Tight triangle of Tory, captain, and soldier
- Hold the space still so the line lands

**Important details:**

- The captain's calm is central here

### Spread 10 (Pages 23-24)

**Text beat:** The Tory watches, then leaves satisfied.

**Visual objective:** Release the pressure slowly.

**Composition ideas:**

- Tory withdrawing through camp
- Soldiers still frozen until he is truly gone

**Important details:**

- Relief should not come too soon

### Spread 11 (Pages 25-26)

**Text beat:** The soldiers wait, then laugh.

**Visual objective:** Let the relief break naturally.

**Composition ideas:**

- Faces finally changing
- Group laughter beginning small, not rowdy

**Important details:**

- This is relief, not mockery

### Spread 12 (Pages 27-28)

**Text beat:** Courage, endurance, and quick thinking.

**Visual objective:** Deliver the manuscript's thematic close.

**Composition ideas:**

- Group scene settling after danger
- Or quieter image with residual honey/chest details

**Important details:**

- If later revised, this spread could become more image-led and less text-led

### Spread 13 (Pages 29-30)

**Text beat:** Honey hidden in a chest.

**Visual objective:** Turn the story object into a symbol.

**Composition ideas:**

- Still life or quiet symbolic scene
- Honey warm against muted camp tones

**Important details:**

- Reduce clutter and let the object speak

### Spread 14 (Pages 31-32)

**Text beat:** Laughter after danger passed.

**Visual objective:** End on release.

**Composition ideas:**

- Simple final image of shared relief
- Could be figures in soft camp light or a quieter symbolic image carrying lightness

**Important details:**

- End with softness rather than moral force

## Research Checklist

- Period camp layout and storage chests
- Handkerchief and clothing details for soldiers
- Cask shape and plausible honey storage
- Bee sting swelling depiction for children
- Tory civilian clothing appropriate to the period

## Open Art Decisions

- Whether the book should lean more suspenseful or slightly warmer and funnier
- Whether Daniel or the captain is the stronger visual center in the inspection scene
- Whether the ending keeps the thematic text or shifts to a quieter image-forward close

- the Tory entering camp
- the chest and cask reveal
- the honey hand / soap line
- the bee-stung soldier moment
- the relieved laughter ending
