# The Cold Room at Leek

## Format

- Working 7-page back-half companion story
- Intended for Canva pages `8-14` in the shared draft
- Same Sieger character anchor and winter courier sketchbook style as `Sieger Rides through the Snow`

## Dummy Layout

### Page 8

Sieger rode his bicycle
to visit a family
who wanted to hear the gospel.

The road was long,
but the visit mattered.

**Illustration note:** Sieger arriving in a quiet Dutch village, same lanky young-man design, same open paper and wind-softened linework.

### Page 9

When he arrived,
he found an old mother
very sick in bed.

The bed should have been
a place of rest.

Instead it was a place of cold.

**Illustration note:** Interior gray light. Bed, rafters, thin wall, cold drifting in. The room should feel plain and exposed rather than melodramatic.

### Page 10

Air came in
where it should not.

The room was exposed.

The weather seemed to press
right through the house.

**Illustration note:** The cold itself becomes the visual force, with white space and diagonal strokes making the room feel penetrated by weather.

### Page 11

No one in that moment
could make her well.

No one could solve
every problem in that home.

But Sieger saw one thing at once:

she should not be left
in such biting cold.

**Illustration note:** Quiet decision spread. Sieger looking up toward the roofline, practical compassion, no theatrical gesture.

### Page 12

So he did not stop
with concern.

He helped.

They found old sacks.

They climbed up
and laid them where they were needed.

**Illustration note:** Roof work in winter air. Old sacks, rough boards, awkward climbing, useful effort. Same spare Dutch sketchbook language.

### Page 13

It was rough work.

It was makeshift work.

It was not beautiful.

It was not permanent.

But it was mercy
you could touch.

**Illustration note:** The patch itself matters. Hands, cloth, roof edge, wind. Practical repair as emotional center.

### Page 14

When Sieger rode away,
the world was still hard.

The woman was still sick.

But the room was warmer.

The bed was kinder.

The house was less cruel than before.

And that mattered.

**Illustration note:** Closing ride-away image with one warmer window or softer interior glow. End on modest visible mercy.
