## Dummy Layout

### Page 1

The news came in winter.

A brother in Arnhem had died.

Sieger was asked to come help.

**Illustration note:** Opening note of winter urgency. Sieger small inside a cold world, with the summons to go already felt.

### Page 2

So he took his bicycle
and set out.

The road was long.

The wind was against him.

**Illustration note:** Strong travel opening. Bicycle, road, winter sky, and the first resistance from the weather.

### Page 3

It pressed on his chest
and shoved at his wheels
as if the weather itself
were saying,
"Go back."

**Illustration note:** Most dramatic weather image in the first half. Sieger pushed by wind, still leaning forward.

### Page 4

But Sieger kept riding.

He rode toward places
he did not know well.

He rode because someone
had asked for help.

**Illustration note:** Determination page. The bike should feel small against the landscape, but Sieger's purpose should read clearly.

### Page 5

By the time he reached Zwolle,
he had another problem.

He did not know the names.

He did not know the streets.

He did not know which door
was the right one.

**Illustration note:** Town-at-dusk uncertainty. Stranger in an unfamiliar place, lantern windows, searching eyes.

### Page 6

So he asked.

One person.

Then another.

Then another.

At last he found the place
and could rest for the night.

**Illustration note:** This page can carry a little humble comedy and relief. Asking, searching, finally arriving.

### Page 7

But morning brought snow.

The world turned white.

The road blurred.

The weather swallowed distance.

**Illustration note:** Whiteout page. Quiet, intense, nearly swallowed by weather.

### Page 8

Sieger could not go on
by bicycle now.

So he left it behind
and took the train.

**Illustration note:** Important turn. The plan changes. Keep the feeling of giving up one path in order to keep going another way.

### Page 9

In Arnhem he did the work
he had come to do.

He stood with grieving people.

He visited the sick.

He helped where help was needed.

**Illustration note:** Service page. Keep it dignified and human, not overly solemn or theatrical.

### Page 10

Then, when the time came,
he turned toward home.

He found his bicycle again.

**Illustration note:** Reset for the return journey. A breath before he faces the road once more.

### Page 11

And once more
he rode into the wind.

**Illustration note:** Strong single-image page. Sieger and bicycle against difficult weather again.

### Page 12

Past strange villages.

Past frozen fields.

Past weariness.

Past doubt.

**Illustration note:** Traveling montage feel, but still one coherent scene. The journey stretching on.

### Page 13

He did not ride
because the road was easy.

He rode because love
had sent him.

**Illustration note:** Emotional summing-up page. Keep it simple and strong.

### Page 14

And love,
when it is needed,
keeps going.

Even through the snow.

**Illustration note:** Closing image should feel quiet, luminous, and earned. End on endurance, not spectacle.
