Working look name:

- `winter courier sketchbook`

## What To Keep

- pen line plus colored pencil on warm white paper
- true `2:1` spread composition for picture-book pages
- artwork that can still fade softly toward the paper at the edges
- visible sketch lines, hand-drawn texture, and whispery unfinished edges
- pale winter palette with blue-gray snow and sky
- open negative space doing the work of weather, silence, distance, and grief
- most detail concentrated around Sieger, the bicycle, and the key human moment
- young-adult grounded character design rather than child proportions or heroic adult bulk
- motion through weather, with the rider leaning into wind and snow
- gentle historical feel without becoming stiff or overly formal
- tiny warm spots of human life such as a lit window, a doorway, or a station lamp
- slightly old children's-book charm, less polished fantasy illustration

## Approved Face And Character Read

The approved render is now the main face and style lock for Sieger.

Keep him reading as:

- slim Dutch young man, around `25`
- earnest face
- slightly formal early-1900s feel
- expressive posture more than exaggerated facial acting
- neat coat, scarf, hat, and boots
- a little windblown and a little under-rested
- sandy to golden hair is good
- longer face
- subtly stronger jaw, brow, and nose than the earlier boyish versions

For Sieger, keep him:

- youthful but clearly adult
- capable but not exaggerated
- lankier and more Dutch/Mr.-Darling-adjacent than the current reference character
- soft and child-friendly, not stiff
- small against the weather and landscape
- face simplified, but now closer to the approved render than the earlier softer studies
- avoid child proportions, round little-boy cheeks, fashion-illustration prettiness, or too much face modeling

## Bicycle Read

The bicycle should be:

- wiry
- slightly whimsical
- more illustrated than mechanically exact
- elegant enough to feel old-fashioned, but not fussy

Avoid:

- overly technical wheel and frame rendering
- realism that pulls focus away from Sieger
- bike geometry that feels modern or clinical

## Storytelling Value

This reference is useful because it balances:

- hardship
- warmth
- motion
- tenderness
- old-fashioned elegance

It makes the winter journey feel difficult, but still inviting enough for a picture book.

## Wind Direction

The wind should feel like a character.

Not a monster.

More like invisible pressure.

Show it through:

- diagonal pencil strokes
- scarf tails and coat hems pushed sideways
- bicycle wheels fighting the slant
- snowy emptiness swallowing roads and towns

## Palette

Mostly use:

- cool blue-gray
- soft graphite
- faded green
- weathered brown
- muted black

Use warmth sparingly:

- amber lamplight
- brick red
- soft cream in faces and paper glow

## Edge Treatment

Keep the airy fade and whispery paper edges, but prioritize wide spread storytelling now over square isolated cutout art.

Ideally:

- far edges can dissolve softly into paper
- background details can fragment into loose marks
- the atmosphere can still feel open and pale
- but the image should read as a finished `2:1` story spread, not a floating square vignette

## Face And Finish

The approved face is close.

Keep pushing toward:

- gentle masculinity
- longer, slimmer adult proportions
- fewer rendered planes in the face than a polished digital painting
- storybook shorthand rather than portrait realism

The figure should feel lovable, competent, and grown, not glamorous.

## Reference Note

There is also a desired kinship with older children's-book drawing styles in the direction of "Rosie's Walk" / classic sketch-led picture-book illustration:

- charming
- economical
- quietly whimsical
- confident line with lots of breathing room

Treat that as a tone reference, not something to imitate directly.

## Book-Wide Trick

- Start with the bicycle feeling nimble and personal.
- Let the weather erase the world more and more.
- By the snow/train section, whole spreads can go nearly white.
- On the ride home, bring back bike tracks and stronger linework so it feels like resolve returning.
