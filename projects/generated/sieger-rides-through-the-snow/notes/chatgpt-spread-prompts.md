## Master Style Block

Use the attached approved reference image as both the character and style anchor. Original children's picture-book illustration, winter courier sketchbook style, pen-and-colored-pencil drawing on warm white paper, true 2:1 horizontal picture-book spread, airy edges that can soften into the paper, lots of open negative space, detail concentrated near the central figure and fading into loose sketch marks at the edges, whispery unfinished borders, soft winter palette of blue-gray, graphite, weathered brown, muted black, and faded green, delicate early-1900s Dutch character design, slim lanky Dutch young man around 25 with sandy-gold hair, earnest face with gentle but slightly masculine features, neat coat and scarf and cap and boots, tender emotional storytelling, wind and snow shown through slanted pencil strokes and drifting white space, whimsical wiry illustrated bicycle rather than a technically exact bicycle, tiny warm accents of human life such as a lit window or station lamp, historical Holland, no text in image.

Dutch details are welcome when they feel natural:

- distant windmills
- low roads and canals
- church spires
- sparse farm buildings

Do not force them into every scene.

## Spread Try 1

### Story Beat

`The wind was against him.`

### Prompt

Use Image 1 as the approved Sieger character and style anchor. Create one original true 2:1 horizontal picture-book spread. Winter courier sketchbook style, pen-and-colored-pencil drawing on warm white paper, airy fading edges, whispery unfinished borders, soft winter palette of blue-gray, graphite, weathered brown, muted black, and faded green. Sieger rides alone on a thin snowy Dutch road, leaning into a hard crosswind. He should match Image 1 in face, age read, silhouette, and clothing spirit: slim lanky Dutch young man around 25, sandy-gold hair, gentle but slightly masculine features, neat dark coat, scarf, cap, and boots, lightly under-rested, soft and child-friendly, not stiff. The wind feels like invisible pressure through diagonal pencil strokes, scarf tails blown sideways, coat hem pushed back, and bicycle wheels fighting the slant. Use a whimsical wiry illustrated bicycle, elegant but not technically exact. Wide white sky and a mostly pale spread, with the rider small against weather and distance. Tiny warm human life far away: one lit farmhouse, church window, or another natural Dutch winter detail if it fits organically. Tender, windswept, determined, historical Holland, no text in image.

### What To Watch For

- Sieger should feel like a slim grown young man, not a boy.
- Keep the page mostly white and let the edges disappear fully.
- Let the wind dominate more than scenery.
- Avoid polished digital painting.
- Avoid a child face or a highly rendered portrait face.
- Avoid a realistic bicycle obsession.

## Spread Try 2

### Story Beat

`So he left it behind and took the train.`

### Prompt

Use Image 1 as the approved Sieger character and style anchor. Create one original true 2:1 horizontal picture-book spread. Winter courier sketchbook style, pen-and-colored-pencil drawing on warm white paper, airy fading edges, whispery unfinished borders, soft winter palette of blue-gray, graphite, weathered brown, muted black, and faded green. Quiet snowy station scene: Sieger has reached the point where he cannot keep riding, and must leave his bicycle behind and take the train. Sieger should match Image 1 in face, age read, silhouette, and clothing spirit: slim lanky Dutch young man around 25, sandy-gold hair, gentle but slightly masculine features, neat dark coat, scarf, cap, and boots. Show the whimsical wiry bicycle resting near a wall or station post like a loyal companion waiting, while Sieger stands nearby in wind and snow, small but resolved. Nearly all white spread, sparse architecture, station lamp or doorway giving one amber point of warmth, loose snow-strokes and unfinished edges, grief and duty held in the silence, tender and lonely rather than dramatic. If Dutch architectural notes fit naturally, they may appear in the station environment, but do not force them.

### What To Watch For

- The bike should feel personal, almost like a secondary character.
- The bike should feel wiry, illustrated, and charming rather than mechanically accurate.
- Keep the station simple and lonely.
- Preserve white space and emotional quiet.
- Make Sieger feel resolved, not defeated.

## Same Scene, Four Style Tests

Use the same underlying scene in each of these tests so we can compare style rather than composition.

### Shared Story Beat

`The wind was against him.`

### Shared Scene Core

Sieger rides alone on a thin snowy Dutch road in historical Holland, leaning into a hard crosswind. He is a slim, lanky Dutch young man around 25 with sandy-gold hair, a gentle face with slightly masculine features, neat dark coat, scarf, cap, and boots. The bicycle should feel wiry, whimsical, elegant, and illustrated rather than mechanically exact. The wind should feel like invisible pressure through diagonal marks, scarf tails blown sideways, coat hem pushed back, and the bicycle fighting the slant. Keep the composition a true 2:1 story spread. No text in image.

### Style Test A

Reference feel: `sref 1465363246`

Antique storybook wash, delicate ink line and faded watercolor on cream paper, muted dusty blue and warm tan palette, elegant old illustrated-book ornament without becoming fussy, soft historical charm, lightly aged paper texture, very gentle line weight, calm decorative storytelling. Keep Sieger simplified and child-friendly, not glamorous. Let the snow and distance dissolve softly into pale paper. Preserve the emotional wind and the bike journey, but interpret them through a refined vintage picture-book lens.

### Style Test B

Reference feel: `sref 1126545584`

Bright stylized pop-anime illustration with bold shape language, clean graphic silhouettes, saturated cyan sky and pink cloud energy translated into a winter version, high contrast, playful color blocking, lively modern character design, expressive but not overly cute. Keep the book scene intact: snowy Dutch road, crosswind, lanky rider, wiry whimsical bike. Use the style's punch and clarity, but hold onto the historical clothing and the emotional solitude of the ride.

### Style Test C

Reference feel: `sref 951959107`

Painterly adventure-book illustration with broad confident shapes, warm-cool contrast, cinematic composition, simplified but appealing faces, textured digital brushwork, stylized environment design, gentle story-game atmosphere. Keep the scene historical and wintry, but let the rider and road feel like a sweeping illustrated journey moment. Sieger should still be slim, earnest, and young, and the bicycle should remain wiry and charming rather than realistic.

### Style Test D

Reference feel: `sref 3498674142`

Soft pastel picture-book style, airy and cozy, powdery textures, pale mint and sky-blue color family adapted into winter whites and cool blue-grays, rounded child-friendly shapes, lots of breathing room, gentle whimsy, tender illustrative simplicity. Keep the same snowy bicycle scene and same emotional stakes, but render it with softer edges, sweeter proportions, and a dreamy hush. Avoid making Sieger too babyish; he should still read as a lanky young Dutch man rather than a teenager.

### Comparison Checklist

- Sieger should still look like the same approved young-man character in each test.
- The underlying bike scene should stay consistent.
- The bike should never become modern, sporty, or technically fussy.
- The wind should remain visible in every version.
- Even the brighter styles should preserve the loneliness and duty of the ride.
