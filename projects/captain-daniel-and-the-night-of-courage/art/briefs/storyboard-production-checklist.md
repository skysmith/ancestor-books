# Storyboard Production Checklist

## Goal

Move Book 1 from manuscript planning into actual visual assembly.

This checklist is for rough storyboard and rough image-comp production, not final illustration polish.

## Start Point

Start image work after these are in place:

- `manuscript/dummy-layout.md`
- `art/briefs/illustration-brief.md`
- `art/briefs/thumbnail-sheet.md`

All three are now present, so storyboard work can begin immediately.

## Phase 1: Thumbnail Pass

- Draw all front matter pages plus all 14 story spreads as small roughs.
- Solve only composition, silhouette, motion, and text-safe zones.
- Keep Daniel's age, hat, and wall scale consistent.
- Mark the six anchor spreads before refining anything else.

## Anchor Spreads To Build First

1. Spread 1: road and walls establishing shot
2. Spread 2: thirty shadows reveal
3. Spread 4: musket flash
4. Spread 7: wall climb
5. Spread 10: firelight wound scene
6. Spread 14: final quiet image

## Phase 2: Rough Image Comps

- Create rough image comps only for the six anchor spreads first.
- Match the painterly historical realism established in the current reference images.
- Keep light logic consistent: moonlight, musket flash, lantern light, firelight.
- Avoid over-detailing costumes or background figures at this stage.
- Test text placement directly on top of the rough comps.

## Phase 3: Full Storyboard Assembly

- Place all thumbnail sketches or rough comps in page order.
- Check page-turn tension from Spread 1 through Spread 8.
- Make sure Spread 10 clearly drops into quiet aftermath.
- Make sure Spread 14 is visually simpler than Spread 13.
- Confirm the book reads clearly on both single-page and full-spread review.

## Phase 4: Revision Pass

- Adjust Daniel's face and age consistency.
- Reduce any graphic blood or injury detail.
- Clarify the enemy read in early spreads without over-explaining them.
- Push the wall taller if the climb feels too easy.
- Simplify any spread where the text competes with high-contrast action.

## Practical Output Targets

- One contact sheet or board showing all spreads in order
- Six stronger rough comps for anchor spreads
- A version with text blocks overlaid for readability check

## Done Criteria For Storyboard Stage

- Every spread has a clear focal point
- Daniel is immediately readable in every scene
- The emotional arc is visible without reading the text
- The hat motif tracks cleanly across the book
- The final spread lands quietly

## What Happens After This

After the storyboard stage is approved, move to either:

1. full roughs for all remaining spreads, or
2. polished key art for the six anchor spreads first
