# Thumbnail Sheet

## Purpose

Rough composition plan for sketching the full 32-page dummy. This is not finished art direction. It is a staging document for fast thumbnails so the pacing, camera movement, and text placement are solved early.

## Reference Alignment

- Use the wall-climb reference image as the primary mood and rendering anchor for the book overall.
- Use the firelight wound reference image as a late-book emotional and lighting reference, especially for Spread 10.
- Keep Daniel visibly young throughout the thumbnail pass.
- Keep blood and wound detail restrained even when referencing the firelight image.

## Global Composition Rules

- Keep text areas quiet and readable; avoid placing faces or key action directly behind type.
- Early spreads should feel compressed and enclosed.
- Middle spreads should accelerate through diagonal movement and stronger contrast.
- Late spreads should widen, settle, and simplify.
- Preserve left-to-right motion whenever possible so Daniel's movement feels propulsive.

## Value Strategy

- Early book: dark masses with narrow light openings
- Action peak: harsh flash, high contrast, broken shapes
- Aftermath: warmer mid-values, more stable shapes
- Ending: simplified values and fewer focal points

## Front Matter Thumbnails

### Page 1: Half-Title

- **Camera:** Close crop
- **Image:** Daniel's hat brim against rough stone, touched by moonlight
- **Value pattern:** Mostly dark with one pale highlight
- **Text-safe area:** Upper right or centered top

### Page 2: Copyright

- **Camera:** Minimal spot image
- **Image:** Small gravel-and-shadow vignette or no art
- **Value pattern:** Very open
- **Text-safe area:** Entire page

### Page 3: Title Page

- **Camera:** Medium-wide
- **Image:** Young Daniel in three-quarter view at the road's edge before events begin
- **Value pattern:** Strong silhouette against dim sky
- **Text-safe area:** Upper left or upper center

### Page 4: Silent Lead-In

- **Camera:** Wide establishing shot
- **Image:** Empty stretch of road between stone walls
- **Value pattern:** Road as lighter band between dark walls
- **Text-safe area:** No text or small opener if needed

## Story Spread Thumbnails

### Spread 1 (Pages 5-6)

- **Camera:** Long shot, low angle
- **Composition:** Road begins lower left and pulls the eye toward center distance; walls rise on both sides
- **Focal point:** Daniel and companion as small figures
- **Motion:** Slow forward walk
- **Value pattern:** Dark walls framing a lighter strip of road and sky
- **Text placement:** Left page upper third and right page upper right
- **Sketch note:** Hide tiny maybe-not-there shadow suggestions in wall shapes

### Spread 2 (Pages 7-8)

- **Camera:** Mid-wide reveal
- **Composition:** Daniel and companion low in frame; silhouettes suddenly crest the wall line above them
- **Focal point:** The rising line of enemy forms
- **Motion:** Upward reveal more than lateral movement
- **Value pattern:** Dark figures against slightly lighter sky
- **Text placement:** Left page lower left or upper left; right page lower right kept clear until reveal lands
- **Sketch note:** Let the gutter help separate Daniel below from danger above

### Spread 3 (Pages 9-10)

- **Camera:** Medium close on Daniel
- **Composition:** Daniel foreground left or center; wall edge and unseen figures crowd him from off-page space
- **Focal point:** Daniel's face and posture
- **Motion:** Stillness under pressure
- **Value pattern:** Face and hat lightly picked out, background mostly obscured
- **Text placement:** Left page upper left; right page upper right with negative space around him
- **Sketch note:** Use cropped gun barrels, hands, or shadowed faces instead of full bodies

### Spread 4 (Pages 11-12)

- **Camera:** Extreme dramatic spread
- **Composition:** Daniel angled across the gutter as musket flash erupts outward and briefly illuminates enemies
- **Focal point:** White-orange blast
- **Motion:** Sharp outward burst
- **Value pattern:** Highest contrast in book
- **Text placement:** Keep lower corners quieter if possible; text should not compete with flash center
- **Sketch note:** Treat flash like a lighting event shaping every object on the spread

### Spread 5 (Pages 13-14)

- **Camera:** Fragmented close action
- **Composition:** Daniel larger in frame; hat strike on left page, side wound and frozen pause on right
- **Focal point:** Torn hat, then Daniel's stunned face/body check
- **Motion:** Violence interrupted by a tiny still beat
- **Value pattern:** Broken flashes and impact accents in dark field
- **Text placement:** Left page bottom or top margin; right page upper right with breathing room around "almost froze"
- **Sketch note:** Consider one continuous scene rather than panels, but stage two impacts clearly

### Spread 6 (Pages 15-16)

- **Camera:** Tracking shot
- **Composition:** Daniel sprinting left to right along the wall, wall looming ahead on right page
- **Focal point:** Daniel's run and the impossible wall
- **Motion:** Strong lateral motion into obstacle
- **Value pattern:** Road streaking as mid-tone band, wall a heavy dark block
- **Text placement:** Left page large quiet area above runner; right page upper left or sky area
- **Sketch note:** Make the leap setup feel instinctive, not planned
- **Reference anchor:** Wall-climb image for palette, urgency, and Daniel's age

### Spread 7 (Pages 17-18)

- **Camera:** Low and steep
- **Composition:** Left page close on hands and stone; right page wider with Daniel suspended atop wall then falling beyond
- **Focal point:** Hands gripping edge, then body in silhouette
- **Motion:** Upward climb followed by downward drop
- **Value pattern:** Strong vertical darks with a lighter sky band at top
- **Text placement:** Left page lower left or upper margin; right page open sky for text
- **Sketch note:** This spread should almost make the reader feel the scrape of stone
- **Reference anchor:** Primary match to the wall-climb image; keep the wall even more imposing

### Spread 8 (Pages 19-20)

- **Camera:** Medium-wide to medium
- **Composition:** Left page Daniel facing shadowy figures with hands up; right page same grouping but lantern light redefines the scene
- **Focal point:** Raised hands on left, familiar faces on right
- **Motion:** Forward panic stopping abruptly
- **Value pattern:** Left page cool and dark, right page warmed by lantern glow
- **Text placement:** Left page upper left; right page upper right or above lantern halo
- **Sketch note:** This is a reveal by light, not by pose change alone

### Spread 9 (Pages 21-22)

- **Camera:** Mid-group scene
- **Composition:** Daniel leaning urgently toward officer; officer and men create a calmer counter-shape
- **Focal point:** Daniel's insistence vs officer's restraint
- **Motion:** Energy pressing forward, then being held back
- **Value pattern:** Lantern pool center, dark night beyond
- **Text placement:** Left page upper left; right page upper right
- **Sketch note:** Keep the officer grounded and steady so refusal reads as judgment

### Spread 10 (Pages 23-24)

- **Camera:** Intimate close-medium
- **Composition:** Daniel seated or half-turned by firelight, hand lifted with blood visible
- **Focal point:** His hand and quiet expression
- **Motion:** Still
- **Value pattern:** Warm firelight on face and hand, soft falloff into darkness
- **Text placement:** Left page upper left; right page mostly open dark or tent space
- **Sketch note:** This spread should exhale after the chase
- **Reference anchor:** Firelight wound image for mood only; soften blood and let expression lead

### Spread 11 (Pages 25-26)

- **Camera:** Wide morning scene
- **Composition:** Men small in landscape near wall; blood drops visible in foreground or mid-ground
- **Focal point:** Daniel registering what the evidence means
- **Motion:** Slow looking, no rush
- **Value pattern:** Pale dawn, flatter contrast, cooler quiet light
- **Text placement:** Left page sky area; right page upper right or pale open space
- **Sketch note:** The world should feel ordinary again, which makes the evidence more sobering

### Spread 12 (Pages 27-28)

- **Camera:** Campfire circle
- **Composition:** Daniel slightly separated within group; others lean inward as he retells story
- **Focal point:** Daniel speaking, others interpreting
- **Motion:** Circular inward attention
- **Value pattern:** Warm fire core against dark perimeter
- **Text placement:** Left page upper left; right page upper right or above firelit heads
- **Sketch note:** Let Daniel's expression stay measured even while others seem impressed

### Spread 13 (Pages 29-30)

- **Camera:** Quiet full-spread portrait/object scene
- **Composition:** Daniel with hat centered low or off-center, background extremely simple
- **Focal point:** Hat with hole
- **Motion:** None
- **Value pattern:** Soft, restrained light with one clear highlight on hat damage
- **Text placement:** Upper third kept very clean
- **Sketch note:** This is the transition from action memory to symbolic memory
- **Reference anchor:** Secondary influence from the firelight image if needed, but push more toward memory than injury

### Spread 14 (Pages 31-32)

- **Camera:** Minimal final image
- **Composition option A:** Hat alone on plain ground or against soft light
- **Composition option B:** Daniel small, walking away down road
- **Focal point:** The emotional residue, not narrative detail
- **Motion:** Gentle recession if using walking figure; stillness if using hat only
- **Value pattern:** Simplest in the book
- **Text placement:** Large quiet field around text
- **Sketch note:** Choose whichever ending image better supports silence

## Thumbnail Workflow

1. Draw all 18 units small: front matter pages plus 14 story spreads.
2. Solve only shape, value, and text placement first.
3. Check that page turns escalate correctly from spreads 1 through 8.
4. Reduce clutter in spreads 10 through 14.
5. Only after that, refine costume and historical details.

## Fast Checks Before Final Sketches

- Can you tell where Daniel is on every spread in under two seconds?
- Is the hat readable before and after the bullet strike?
- Do spreads 4 through 7 feel like the visual peak?
- Does spread 14 feel calmer than spread 13?
- Are text areas protected from busy contrast?
- Does Daniel look like the same young person in both action and aftermath scenes?
