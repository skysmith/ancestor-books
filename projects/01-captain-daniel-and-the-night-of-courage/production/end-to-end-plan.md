# End-To-End Plan

## Goal

Take Book 1 from manuscript and art direction through a full rough dummy that is ready for review, revision, and eventual final illustration.

## Production Path

1. Lock the manuscript pacing in `manuscript/dummy-layout.md`.
2. Lock visual language in `art/briefs/illustration-brief.md`.
3. Lock composition intent in `art/briefs/thumbnail-sheet.md`.
4. Generate per-spread prompt sheets and storyboard boards.
5. Review the six anchor spreads first:
   - Spread 1
   - Spread 2
   - Spread 4
   - Spread 7
   - Spread 10
   - Spread 14
6. Revise Daniel continuity, wall scale, and injury restraint.
7. Approve the full rough dummy as a story object, not final art.
8. Generate or paint higher-fidelity roughs for all spreads.
9. Assemble a second dummy PDF with improved art and text placement.
10. Finalize end matter, cover, and print/export specs.

## Current Pass

This pass focuses on steps 4-7:

- prompt sheets
- rough storyboard images
- contact sheet
- reviewable dummy PDF

## Review Questions

- Does the story escalate clearly without explanation?
- Does Daniel stay young and recognizable throughout?
- Do Spreads 4-7 feel like the visual peak?
- Does Spread 10 slow down correctly?
- Does the final spread land quietly?

## After Review

After this pass is reviewed, the next production choice is:

1. generate stronger rough images spread by spread, or
2. go directly to polished key art for the anchor spreads before filling the rest.
