### Spread 1 (Pages 5-6)

Left:
Before he was a captain,
before years had lined his face,
Daniel Cook was a young soldier walking a dark road.

Right:
The moon slipped behind the clouds.
Wind pressed against the tall stone walls on either side.

Even the night seemed to be waiting.

### Spread 2 (Pages 7-8)

Left:
Daniel and one other soldier had been sent ahead to scout.

"Quiet," Daniel whispered.

Their boots crunched on gravel.

Right:
Then the crunching stopped.

Thirty shadows rose slowly from behind the wall.

### Spread 3 (Pages 9-10)

Left:
"Who comes there?" a voice called from the dark.

Daniel swallowed.

"Friend," he answered.

Right:
"Friend to who?" the voice demanded.

Daniel's heart pounded hard against his ribs.

"Friend to the States," he said, holding his ground.

### Spread 4 (Pages 11-12)

Left:
"Surrender!" the shadows shouted.

In that instant, Daniel moved.

He lifted his musket.
He aimed at the largest figure.
He fired.

Right:
The flash split the darkness.

For one frozen heartbeat, the world stood still.

Then it exploded.

### Spread 5 (Pages 13-14)

Left:
Shots cracked against stone.
A bullet tore through Daniel's hat.
He felt the wind brush his hair.

Right:
Another shot burned along his side.
Hot. Sharp. Close.

For a single breath, he almost froze.

### Spread 6 (Pages 15-16)

Left:
Instead--

he ran.

Boots pounding.
Breath ragged.
Bullets chasing him through the dark.

Right:
The stone wall ahead looked taller than before.

"Climb," his mind urged.

He leapt.

### Spread 7 (Pages 17-18)

Left:
Rough stone scraped his palms.
A shot struck inches from his hand.

He pulled himself up and over.

Right:
For one suspended moment, he hung between sky and earth.

Then he dropped hard on the other side and ran down the hill.

### Spread 8 (Pages 19-20)

Left:
Figures loomed ahead.

Daniel threw up his hands.

"I give up!" he shouted.

Right:
The words felt strange in his mouth.

Lantern light flickered across familiar faces.

His own men.

### Spread 9 (Pages 21-22)

Left:
Relief rushed through him so suddenly his knees nearly gave way.

He urged them forward.
"The enemy is just beyond the wall!"

Right:
But the officer shook his head.

"It is too dangerous in the dark. We return to camp."

### Spread 10 (Pages 23-24)

Left:
Back by the firelight, Daniel reached for his side.

His hand came away red.

He searched for the wound.

Right:
The cut was small.

He said nothing.

### Spread 11 (Pages 25-26)

Left:
The next morning, they returned to the wall.

In the pale light of dawn, they found drops of blood on the ground.

Right:
"Looks like your shot found its mark," one of the men said.

Daniel nodded.

He did not feel triumphant.

### Spread 12 (Pages 27-28)

Left:
That night, the soldiers asked him to tell the story again.

He spoke of shadows.
Of gunfire.
Of the climb over stone.

Right:
They called it bravery.

### Spread 13 (Pages 29-30)

Daniel kept the hat with the hole in it--

### Spread 14 (Pages 31-32)

a reminder

of the night he ran forward
when every shot told him to turn back.
