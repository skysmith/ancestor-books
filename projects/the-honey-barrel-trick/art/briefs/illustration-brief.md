# Illustration Brief

## Project

**Title:** Captain Daniel and the Honey Barrel  
**Format:** 32-page picture book dummy  
**Audience:** Picture-book readers; suspenseful, historical, and lightly comic  
**Story function:** A combined honey-arc story joining hunger, stealth, quick thinking, and relief

## Visual Goal

This version should feel like the broadest and most cinematic honey book in the set. It needs to hold both the hunger-driven mission and the near-discovery scene in one continuous arc.

The art should balance night stealth and indoor/camp-close suspense without becoming visually crowded.

## Overall Art Direction

- Use painterly historical realism with clear staging.
- Let honey, hand, chest, and watchful faces become the main visual anchors.
- Keep Daniel young and emotionally readable.
- Build tension through proximity and silence rather than exaggerated expression.
- Let the book shift from cold night stealth into tighter, more intimate suspicion.
- Keep night scenes brighter and more legible than near-black cinematic night; use moonlight, reflected winter light, doorway glow, or lantern spill so action reads immediately.
- Push spread-to-spread variety so the approach, honey reveal, lie, scrutiny, and relief each have distinct composition and camera distance.

## Tone And Pacing

- Early spreads: hunger and quiet mission
- Middle spreads: discovery of honey and sudden danger
- Late spreads: quick lie, redirected suspicion, relief
- Ending: light thematic release

## Character Notes

### Daniel Cook

- Young, quick, and dependable
- Less physically dominant than in Book 1
- Should feel like a natural part of the group response

### Tory

- Suspicious and present enough to create pressure
- Not cartoonish

### Captain

- Calm and capable when the danger shifts into questioning

### Other Soldiers

- Important as a nervous group presence
- Their faces and posture can help carry suspense

## Setting Notes

- Night approach to a Tory house
- Interior or edge-of-house search area where the cask is found
- Bee-sting aftermath and scrutiny at close range
- Overall space should remain legible despite the combined story arc

## Palette And Light

- Opening: cold blue-gray night
- Mid-book: warm honey gold against darker surroundings
- Late-book: tighter pool of light around faces and props
- Ending: softened warmth after release

## Key Visual Motifs

- Honey glow
- Handkerchief and honey-covered hand
- Mouths and expressions held in tension
- Cask, chest, and small concealed spaces

## Spread Focus

- Spread 2: night approach
- Spread 5: honey-covered hand
- Spread 7: soap line and Daniel's answer
- Spread 9: bee-stung soldier
- Spread 10: toothache line and held silence
- Spread 12: relief and laughter

## Deliverable Recommendation

Create a thumbnail sheet that solves:

- the mission approach
- the honey close-up
- the lie under pressure
- the bee-sting redirect
- the laugh release
