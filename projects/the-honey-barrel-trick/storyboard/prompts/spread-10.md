## First-Pass Prompt

Painterly historical realism for a children's Revolutionary War picture book. Young Daniel Cook, visibly teenage and vulnerable rather than heroic, in blue-gray night tones with warm firelight or musket flashes where appropriate. Scene focus: The captain answered calmly. "He has the toothache." For a long moment the Tory studied them. No one moved. No one spoke. Illustration direction: stillness and eye-lines carry the tension. Make this a tight scrutiny scene with grouped faces and pressure in the pause, not a repeated wide scene. Keep the low light brighter and more legible on faces. Text-safe composition, emotionally clear faces, textured brushwork, subtle period costume detail, no graphic violence.

## Continuity Reminders

- Daniel remains visibly young across the entire book.
- Keep his hat consistent until the bullet hole becomes important later.
- Use painterly brushwork and historical realism, not slick digital rendering.
- Preserve room for text; avoid placing faces behind main copy blocks.
