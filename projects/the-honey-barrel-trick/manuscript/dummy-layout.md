# Captain Daniel and the Honey Barrel

## Format

- Standard 32-page picture book dummy
- Front matter assumption:
  - Page 1: half-title
  - Page 2: copyright
  - Page 3: title page
  - Page 4: blank or opening illustration
- Story begins on page 5

## Dummy Layout

### Spread 1 (Pages 5-6)

**Left Page**

The soldiers were still hungry.

Winter had been long.

**Right Page**

Food was hard to find.

But the captain had given them an order.

### Spread 2 (Pages 7-8)

**Left Page**

"Go out and take some Tory's chickens," he said,
"or anything you can find."

**Right Page**

So Daniel Cook and several soldiers
went quietly through the dark.

**Illustration note:** Night movement toward the Tory property, hunger already established.

### Spread 3 (Pages 9-10)

**Left Page**

They came to the house
of a Tory.

Inside the house
were supplies the soldiers badly needed.

**Right Page**

But they had to be careful.

If the Tory discovered them,
everything would be lost.

### Spread 4 (Pages 11-12)

**Left Page**

The soldiers moved quickly
and quietly.

One of them lifted the lid
of a small cask.

**Right Page**

Inside
was honey.

### Spread 5 (Pages 13-14)

**Left Page**

Without thinking,
he plunged his hand inside.

The honey was thick and sticky.

**Right Page**

When he pulled his hand back out,
it was covered.

The soldier quickly slammed the lid down.

**Illustration note:** Strong close-up spread, honey hand and sudden panic.

### Spread 6 (Pages 15-16)

**Left Page**

Just then
the Tory appeared.

**Right Page**

The soldier grabbed his handkerchief
and began wiping the honey
from his hand.

### Spread 7 (Pages 17-18)

**Left Page**

Then he said loudly,

"Boys, what in thunder
do you keep soap here for?"

**Right Page**

Daniel answered at once.

"We have to keep it there
or the soldiers will steal it from us."

### Spread 8 (Pages 19-20)

**Left Page**

The Tory looked puzzled.

But he seemed satisfied.

**Right Page**

Then he noticed
one of the soldiers.

### Spread 9 (Pages 21-22)

**Left Page**

The man stood
with his mouth wide open.

His tongue was swollen
from the sting of a bee.

**Right Page**

The Tory pointed at him.

"What ails that man?"

**Illustration note:** Tension redirects to the bee-stung soldier.

### Spread 10 (Pages 23-24)

**Left Page**

The captain answered calmly.

"He has the toothache."

**Right Page**

For a long moment
the Tory studied them.

No one moved.

No one spoke.

### Spread 11 (Pages 25-26)

**Left Page**

Then the old man nodded
and turned away.

**Right Page**

The soldiers waited
until he was gone.

### Spread 12 (Pages 27-28)

**Left Page**

Then they looked at one another.

And laughed.

**Right Page**

War was full of danger.

Sometimes it was full of hunger.

### Spread 13 (Pages 29-30)

**Full Spread**

But now and then,

a little cleverness--

**Illustration note:** Quiet symbolic spread, honey, chest, and relieved soldiers or a simple still-life.

### Spread 14 (Pages 31-32)

**Full Spread**

could save the day.

**Illustration note:** Minimal final image, lightness after narrow danger.

## Why This Layout Works

- The book keeps both the theft and the inspection in a single arc.
- The honey hand, soap line, and toothache line all get strong page-turn placement.
- The ending releases tension through laughter before the thematic close.

## Possible Enhancement

If later revision wants a quieter close, Spread 12 could end on "And laughed," leaving the final spreads image-led.
