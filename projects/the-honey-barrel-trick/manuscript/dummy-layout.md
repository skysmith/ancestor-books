# Captain Daniel and the Tory's Honey

## Format

- Standard 32-page picture book dummy
- Front matter assumption:
  - Page 1: half-title
  - Page 2: copyright
  - Page 3: title page
  - Page 4: blank or opening illustration
- Story begins on page 5

## Dummy Layout

### Spread 1 (Pages 5-6)

**Left Page**

Daniel Cook and the soldiers
in his company
had been hungry for many days.

**Right Page**

Winter had been hard.

Food was scarce in camp.

### Spread 2 (Pages 7-8)

**Left Page**

At last their captain gave the order.

"Go out and take some Tory's chickens," he said,
"or anything you can find."

**Right Page**

The soldiers knew of a Tory nearby
who had food.

**Illustration note:** Pivot from hunger into the mission, with the captain's order landing clearly.

### Spread 3 (Pages 9-10)

**Left Page**

But the man guarded his house carefully.

A large bulldog
watched the yard.

**Right Page**

No one could come near in the night
without waking the beast.

So the soldiers made a plan.

**Illustration note:** Tory house and bulldog established as the central threat.

### Spread 4 (Pages 11-12)

**Left Page**

Daniel Cook
would stand guard.

**Right Page**

The others would slip quietly
toward the barns and the bee house
to see what food they might find.

### Spread 5 (Pages 13-14)

**Left Page**

The night was dark.

The air was cold.

Daniel walked carefully
toward the house.

**Right Page**

For a moment
the yard was still.

**Illustration note:** Quiet suspense before the dog attack.

### Spread 6 (Pages 15-16)

**Left Page**

Then the dog came.

It rushed from the darkness
in a great fury.

**Right Page**

Daniel saw at once
he must defend himself.

**Illustration note:** Major action spread, bulldog bursting from darkness toward Daniel.

### Spread 7 (Pages 17-18)

**Left Page**

He raised his musket
and fired.

The dog fell.

**Right Page**

The shot brought the Tory
running to the door in anger.

### Spread 8 (Pages 19-20)

**Left Page**

He demanded to know
why the soldier had fired.

**Right Page**

Daniel answered calmly.

**Illustration note:** Daniel steady in cold light while the angry Tory confronts him from the doorway.

### Spread 9 (Pages 21-22)

**Left Page**

He told the man
he had shot the dog
because every time he stood guard
he was in danger
of being torn to pieces.

**Right Page**

The old Tory
scolded and argued.

**Illustration note:** Tense dialogue spread; Daniel calm, Tory heated.

### Spread 10 (Pages 23-24)

**Left Page**

Daniel stood with him
in the cold
and listened.

**Right Page**

After some time
the old man turned
and went back inside.

**Illustration note:** Delay and endurance, the real work happening off-page.

### Spread 11 (Pages 25-26)

**Left Page**

When Daniel returned to camp
he discovered something.

**Right Page**

While the Tory had been arguing with him
outside...

### Spread 12 (Pages 27-28)

**Left Page**

the other soldiers
had slipped
into the bee house.

**Right Page**

And they had taken honey.

But the story
was not finished.

### Spread 13 (Pages 29-30)

**Full Spread**

The next morning
the old Tory came to the army camp
looking for his stolen honey.

**Illustration note:** New suspense phase begins in daylight or cold morning camp light.

### Spread 14 (Pages 31-32)

**Full Spread**

The captain opened the chest.
Honey gleamed on a soldier's hand.
The old Tory watched.
Daniel answered quickly.
The captain said,
"He has the toothache."

Then the old man left.

And the soldiers looked at one another.

And laughed.

**Illustration note:** Compression ending spread: chest, honey, suspicion, and final release all resolved in one strong closing image.

## Why This Layout Works

- The book now preserves the full historical arc as one continuous episode.
- The bulldog attack and the camp-search sequence read as parts of the same adventure.
- The story moves from hunger, to danger, to distraction, to wit.
- It gives Book 3 a complete plot rather than a partial chapter feeling.
