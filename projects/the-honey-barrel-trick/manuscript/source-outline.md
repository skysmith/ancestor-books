## Extracted Source Passage

At another time want [hunger] had driven the company he was in almost to desperation. Their horse beef was all gone and no dogs or any kind of meat could be procured, and they had even roasted their shoes and eaten them. At last their Captain told them to go out and take some Tory's chickens or anything they could find. They knew of a Tory nearby who had plenty but would sell none. He had a large bulldog that guarded the house so there was no coming around in the night. At length a plan was hit upon.

Grandfather was selected as guard, so he went forth near to the house when out came the dog in a great fury. He saw that he must defend himself so he drew his musket and shot the dog, which brought the old Tory to the door in a great rage wanting to know what he had shot for. Grandfather said he had shot his dog for every time he was on duty he was in danger of being torn to pieces, and after detaining the old man for some time in the cold he left for camp and found the boys had got some honey which they had taken from the old Tory's bee house while he was scolding about the dog.

The next morning the old man came to camp in pursuit [of his stolen honey]. The captain told him he did not think his boys had it. But, says he, we will search. So around the camp they went, the old man peeking and looking very closely until he came to the mess where it was hidden in a chest. The captain opened the chest. Putting his hand into a small cask and taking it suddenly out again covered with honey, he slammed down the lid. Taking his handkerchief from his pocket and wiping it off, he said, "Boys what in thunder do you keep soap here for?"

Grandfather said, "We have to keep it there or the soldiers will steal it from us."

One of the soldiers had his tongue swollen so that his mouth was wide open caused by the sting of a bee. The old Tory wondered at this and asked what ailed that man. The captain told him he had the toothache. This passed off first rate and the old man went away.

## Submitted Draft

Captain Daniel and the Honey Barrel

The soldiers were still hungry.

Winter had been long.

Food was hard to find.

But the captain had given them an order.

"Go out and take some Tory's chickens," he said,  
"or anything you can find."

So Daniel Cook and several soldiers went quietly through the dark.

They came to the house of a Tory.

Inside the house were supplies the soldiers badly needed.

But they had to be careful.

If the Tory discovered them, everything would be lost.

The soldiers moved quickly and quietly.

One of them lifted the lid of a small cask.

Inside was honey.

Without thinking, he plunged his hand inside.

The honey was thick and sticky.

When he pulled his hand back out, it was covered.

The soldier quickly slammed the lid down.

Just then the Tory appeared.

The soldier grabbed his handkerchief and began wiping the honey from his hand.

Then he said loudly,

"Boys, what in thunder do you keep soap here for?"

Daniel answered at once.

"We have to keep it there or the soldiers will steal it from us."

The Tory looked puzzled.

But he seemed satisfied.

Then he noticed one of the soldiers.

The man stood with his mouth wide open.

His tongue was swollen from the sting of a bee.

The Tory pointed at him.

"What ails that man?"

The captain answered calmly.

"He has the toothache."

For a long moment the Tory studied them.

No one moved.

No one spoke.

Then the old man nodded and turned away.

The soldiers waited until he was gone.

Then they looked at one another.

And laughed.

War was full of danger.

Sometimes it was full of hunger.

But now and then,

a little cleverness

could save the day.
