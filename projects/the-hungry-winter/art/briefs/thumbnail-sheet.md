# Thumbnail Sheet

## Purpose

Rough composition plan for sketching the full 32-page dummy. This sheet solves mood, value, pacing, and text placement before any detailed image generation or illustration work.

## Global Composition Rules

- Keep compositions sparse; this book needs visual austerity.
- Protect text areas with simple sky, smoke, snow, or dark negative space.
- Let repetition and variation in posture do more work than complicated staging.
- Early and middle spreads should feel heavy and slow.
- The back half should become quieter, not more explosive.

## Value Strategy

- Opening: broad cold mid-tones with very little warmth
- Hardship peak: stark contrast around firelight and worn objects
- Mission section: darker, tighter, more silent
- Ending: simplified shapes and distant figures

## Thumbnail Workflow

1. Draw all front matter pages plus the 14 story spreads small.
2. Solve scale, weather, and text-safe zones first.
3. Check that Spread 4 feels like hardship peak.
4. Check that Spread 6 acts as the structural pivot.
5. Check that the last three spreads grow quieter rather than larger.

## Fast Checks Before Final Sketches

- Does the cold read instantly?
- Does the camp feel increasingly worn down?
- Does the captain's quote clearly pivot the book?
- Does Daniel's guard scene feel humble rather than dramatic?
- Does the final spread end in endurance rather than triumph?
