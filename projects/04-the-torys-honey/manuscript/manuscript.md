# Captain Daniel and the Tory's Honey

## Status

Active manuscript draft.

## Story Draft

Daniel Cook and the soldiers in his company
had been hungry for many days.

Winter had been hard.

Food was scarce in camp.

At last their captain gave the order.

"Go out and take some Tory's chickens," he said,
"or anything you can find."

The soldiers knew of a Tory nearby
who had food.

But the man guarded his house carefully.

A large bulldog watched the yard.

No one could come near in the night
without waking the beast.

So the soldiers made a plan.

Daniel Cook would stand guard.

The others would slip quietly
toward the barns and the bee house
to see what food they might find.

The night was dark.

The air was cold.

Daniel walked carefully toward the house.

For a moment
the yard was still.

Then the dog came.

It rushed from the darkness
in a great fury.

Daniel saw at once
he must defend himself.

He raised his musket
and fired.

The dog fell.

The shot brought the Tory
running to the door in anger.

He demanded to know
why the soldier had fired.

Daniel answered calmly.

He told the man
he had shot the dog
because every time he stood guard
he was in danger of being torn to pieces.

The old Tory scolded and argued.

Daniel stood with him in the cold
and listened.

After some time
the old man turned and went back inside.

When Daniel returned to camp
he discovered something.

While the Tory had been arguing with him
outside...

the other soldiers had slipped
into the bee house.

And they had taken honey.

But the story was not finished.

The next morning
the old Tory came to the army camp.

He was looking
for his stolen honey.

The captain listened carefully.

"I do not think my boys have it," he said.

"But we will search."

So they walked through the camp together.

The Tory looked everywhere.

He peered into tents.

He examined boxes.

He searched the soldiers' mess.

At last he came to a chest.

Inside was a small cask.

The captain opened it.

A soldier put his hand inside.

He pulled it out suddenly,
covered in honey.

Then he slammed down the lid.

Taking his handkerchief from his pocket,
he wiped his hand and said,

"Boys what in thunder do you keep soap here for?"

Daniel answered quickly.

"We have to keep it there
or the soldiers will steal it from us."

The old Tory watched them carefully.

Just then he noticed another soldier.

The man stood
with his mouth wide open.

His tongue was swollen
from the sting of a bee.

The Tory pointed at him.

"What ails that man?"

The captain answered calmly.

"He has the toothache."

For a long moment
the Tory studied the soldiers.

Then he nodded.

Satisfied,
he turned and left the camp.

The soldiers waited
until he was gone.

Then they looked at one another.

And laughed.

War was full of danger.

Sometimes it was full of hunger.

And sometimes,

a little quick thinking

was enough.
