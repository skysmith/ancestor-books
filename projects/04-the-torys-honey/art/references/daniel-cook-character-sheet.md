# Daniel Cook Character Sheet

Use this character specification whenever generating illustrations for the Daniel Cook children's book series.

The goal is to maintain a consistent visual identity across all spreads.

## Core Character Identity

- Name: Daniel Cook
- Age: approximately 16 years old
- Build: lean, wiry, quick rather than muscular
- Height: slightly shorter than most adult soldiers
- Posture: alert, cautious, often slightly hunched from cold or tension

Daniel should look like a young soldier, not a heroic adult officer.

He should feel capable but still visibly young and vulnerable.

## Face

- Face shape: narrow oval
- Jaw: soft, not square
- Cheekbones: light but visible
- Skin tone: light, slightly wind-reddened from cold weather
- Expression baseline: thoughtful, alert, sometimes anxious

### Eyes

- dark brown
- observant
- often scanning surroundings

### Eyebrows

- slightly thick
- expressive but not exaggerated

### Nose

- straight and modest in size

### Mouth

- narrow
- often slightly parted when worried or breathing hard

### Hair

- dark brown
- slightly messy
- medium length
- often damp or pushed under hat

Daniel should look recognizably like the same boy in every image.

## Hat

Type: worn black tricorne hat

### Details

- felt or wool texture
- slightly bent or weathered edges
- small bullet hole on the left side of the hat

The hat with the bullet hole is a recurring visual symbol from Book 1.

The hat should appear in most scenes.

## Clothing

Daniel wears clothing typical of a young Continental soldier or militia member.

Because many early Revolutionary soldiers wore civilian-style clothing rather than standardized uniforms, his outfit should look practical and slightly mismatched rather than formal.

### Base Outfit

#### Shirt

- off-white linen
- slightly loose
- worn and dirt-stained

#### Outer Layer

- tan or buff hunting shirt or rough homespun coat
- simple and practical

#### Chest Strap

- leather strap for powder horn or cartridge box

#### Breeches

- brown or buff knee breeches

#### Stockings

- wool stockings

#### Shoes

- dark leather buckle shoes

### Additional Items Occasionally Visible

- powder horn
- cartridge box
- rolled blanket or bedroll
- small haversack

## Weapons and Gear

Primary weapon: flintlock musket

### Appearance

- long wooden stock
- iron barrel
- simple strap

### Gear Condition

- worn
- practical
- not polished or ceremonial

## Physical Mannerisms

Daniel often:

- leans forward when listening
- keeps one hand near the musket
- scans the darkness
- pauses before acting
- shows quiet determination rather than bravado

His courage should appear through decisions and posture, not exaggerated facial emotion.

## Lighting and Mood Rules

Daniel should always remain visually readable in night scenes.

Typical lighting sources:

- moonlight
- lantern light
- campfire glow
- doorway light
- pale dawn light

Avoid:

- fully dark silhouettes
- faces hidden in shadow
- action-movie lighting

## Character Consistency Rule

When generating images:

- maintain consistent facial structure
- maintain same hair color and style
- maintain same hat and clothing
- maintain Daniel's youthfulness

Daniel must appear as the same identifiable young soldier in every spread.

## Emotional Tone

Daniel is brave but not fearless.

Common emotional states:

- alert
- cautious
- tense
- quietly determined
- occasionally relieved

Avoid heroic exaggeration or triumphant expressions.

The character should feel human, thoughtful, and grounded.

## Visual Motifs Associated With Daniel

Recurring visual elements tied to Daniel:

- tricorne hat with bullet hole
- musket
- winter night air
- watchful posture
- hands holding musket, honey, or wounds

These motifs help children recognize him across scenes.

## Generation Reminder

Maintain continuity with previous illustrations of Daniel Cook.

Do not redesign the character between spreads.

Daniel should look like the same young soldier throughout the entire book.
