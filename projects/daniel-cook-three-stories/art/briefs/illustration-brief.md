# Illustration Brief

## Visual Goal

Painterly historical realism for a children's Revolutionary War picture book. Daniel should feel young, vulnerable, and steady, not like a polished adult hero.

## Daniel Continuity

- Teenage soldier, roughly 15-18.
- Slim build, narrow youthful face, clean-shaven.
- Dark hair under a worn tricorn hat when appropriate.
- Brave, but never fearless.
- Often cold, tired, hungry, or under pressure.

## Tone

The book has dangerous material: gunfire, hunger, a guard dog being shot, desertion charges, and threat of death. Handle all of it with restraint.

- No graphic wounds.
- Do not linger on the dead dog.
- Treat hunger as physical and serious, not comic.
- Let humor in the honey scene come from timing and relief, not slapstick.
- The court-martial should feel frightening but not melodramatic.

## Palette

- Night scouting: blue-gray moonlight, stone, hard white musket flash.
- Hungry camp: cold browns, winter grays, low firelight.
- Honey trick: cold exterior night plus small amber honey accents.
- Pork ending: warmer campfire, but still modest and tired.

## New Art Priorities

The archived art likely covers most of the night scouting and honey scenes. New generation should focus first on:

1. Daniel leaving camp to get pork.
2. Daniel returning with pork on his back.
3. Daniel standing before officers at court-martial.
4. Daniel dividing pork among hungry soldiers.

If production time is tight, combine 2 and 3 into a single spread: Daniel's pork load visible in camp while officers confront him.
