# Daniel Cook: Three Stories from the Revolution

## Status

Fresh combined-book starting point.

This project combines three family-history anecdotes about Daniel Cook into one 24-page children's picture book dummy:

- being fired at by the British while scouting at night
- the Tory's honey trick during a hungry winter
- the pork trip, court-martial, and shared feast

## Working Shape

- `manuscript/manuscript.md` contains the current read-aloud manuscript draft.
- `manuscript/dummy-layout.md` contains the 24-page page-by-page dummy.
- `manuscript/source-outline.md` preserves the source beats and historical constraints.
- `art/existing-image-candidates.md` calls out archived art that may be reusable or useful as reference.
- `art/briefs/illustration-brief.md` gives visual continuity guidance for new images.

## Editorial Direction

The three stories can flow as one book if the spine is not "three adventures" but "three kinds of courage during hunger and war":

- courage under fire
- courage under pressure
- courage under judgment

The tone should stay true to the family account while remaining readable for children: tense but not grim, honest but not graphic, and quietly amazed that Daniel lived through these things.
