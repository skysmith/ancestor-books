## Page 1

Daniel Cook

Three Stories from the Revolution

## Page 2

Only a little was remembered of Daniel Cook.

That little was heard from father to child, and carried on.

## Page 3

He served through the Revolutionary War with England.

The war broke his constitution, and his health was always poor.

He was in many battles and skirmishes.

But still, he was saved.

## Page 4

One night, Daniel was sent out with one other soldier as a scouting party, to see what they could find.

## Page 5

The night was dark.

As they went down a hill, a stone wall stood on both sides of the road.

## Page 6

Then, on the other side, about thirty men rose up from behind the wall.

Because they were higher than Daniel was, he could see them against the sky.

## Page 7

"Who comes there?"

"Friend," Daniel answered.

"Friend to who?"

"Friend to the States."

Then the enemy shouted, "Surrender!"

## Page 8

At that moment, Daniel fired his musket at the biggest one and ran for the opposite wall.

## Page 9

The bullets came like hailstones.

One went through the top of his hat.

One went through his ear.

One grazed his side.

One went into the heel of his shoe.

## Page 10

Down the hill they ran until they met a party of their own men.

Thinking he had met another enemy, Daniel cried, "I give up! I give up!"

Soon, to his joy, he found they were friends.

## Page 11

Daniel wished to pursue the enemy, but the officer thought it was not prudent in the night.

At camp, Daniel tried to find a hole in his side.

His hand was covered with blood, but he found no bullet hole.

In the morning, they found blood on the ground.

## Page 12

At another time, hunger drove Daniel's company almost to desperation.

Their horse beef was gone.

No dogs, or any kind of meat, could be found.

## Page 13

Some of the men even roasted their shoes and ate them.

At last, the captain told them to go out and take some Tory's chickens, or anything they could find.

## Page 14

They knew of a Tory nearby who had plenty, but would sell none.

He had a large bulldog that guarded the house, so there was no coming near in the night.

## Page 15

At length, a plan was made.

Daniel was selected as guard.

He went near the house.

## Page 16

Out came the dog in a great fury.

Daniel saw that he must defend himself.

He drew his musket and fired.

## Page 17

The shot brought the old Tory to the door in a great rage.

Daniel said the dog had put him in danger of being torn to pieces every time he was on duty.

He kept the old man there in the cold for some time.

## Page 18

When Daniel returned to camp, the boys had got some honey.

They had taken it from the old Tory's bee house while the Tory was scolding about the dog.

## Page 19

The next morning, the old Tory came to camp in pursuit.

The captain told him, "I do not think my boys have it."

"But," said the captain, "we will search."

## Page 20

At last they came to the chest where the honey was hidden.

The captain put his hand into a small cask and took it out covered with honey.

Then he slammed down the lid, wiped his hand, and said, "Boys, what in thunder do you keep soap here for?"

## Page 21

Daniel answered, "We have to keep it there or the soldiers will steal it from us."

One of the mess had his tongue swollen from the sting of a bee.

"What ails that man?"

"He has the toothache," the captain said.

Then the old Tory went away.

## Page 22

Just before Lafayette came, the men were almost starved.

Daniel heard news from home that there was pork there.

He asked the captain to let him go home and get some.

The captain said he could not spare him.

But Daniel resolved to go.

## Page 23

That night, Daniel started.

He was gone eight or ten days.

Then he came back with a load of pork on his back.

But he had left without permission.

Daniel was taken for deserting, court-martialed, and found guilty.

## Page 24

Asked why the sentence of death should not be passed, Daniel said he had suffered enough to die a thousand deaths.

If they wished to kill him for what he had done, they might try it.

But he would fight his way through, or lose his life.

The officers consulted.

Finally, Daniel was liberated.

He divided the pork all around, and they had a good feast.
