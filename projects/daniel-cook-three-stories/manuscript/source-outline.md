# Source Outline

## Source Frame

The source voice says Daniel Cook served through the Revolutionary War with England, that the war broke his constitution, and that his health was poor afterward. The teller remembers only a little, passed down through family memory, and preserves three anecdotes.

For the children's book, the most useful frame is:

- Daniel was young when these things happened.
- The war was not only battles. It was hunger, cold, fear, and hard decisions.
- The stories show Daniel surviving by courage, steadiness, quick thinking, and stubborn loyalty to the men around him.

## Story 1: Fired At By The British

Essential beats:

- Daniel and one other soldier are sent out at night as scouts.
- They descend a hill with stone walls on both sides of the road.
- About thirty enemy soldiers rise up behind the wall.
- The enemy challenges them: "Who comes there?"
- Daniel answers, "Friend."
- When asked "Friend to who?" he answers, "Friend to the States."
- The enemy orders them to surrender.
- Daniel fires at the largest figure and runs for the opposite wall.
- Bullets come "like hailstones."
- One bullet goes through the top of his hat.
- One goes through his ear, one grazes his side, and one goes into the heel of his shoe.
- He runs downhill and meets his own men, but thinks he has met another enemy.
- He cries, "I give up! I give up!"
- He wants to return and pursue the enemy, but the officer will not risk it in the dark.
- He fears he has been hit, finds blood, but no bullet hole in his side.
- In the morning they return and find blood on the ground, supposing Daniel likely hit his mark.

## Story 2: The Tory's Honey

Essential beats:

- Hunger has driven the company almost to desperation.
- Horse beef is gone.
- No meat can be found.
- They have roasted and eaten shoes.
- The captain tells the men to take Tory chickens or anything they can find.
- They know a Tory nearby who has plenty but will sell none.
- A large bulldog guards the house.
- Daniel is selected as guard or decoy.
- The dog rushes out.
- Daniel sees he must defend himself and shoots the dog.
- The old Tory comes to the door in a rage.
- Daniel keeps him arguing in the cold.
- While Daniel delays him, the other soldiers take honey from the bee house.
- The next morning the old Tory comes to camp searching for the honey.
- The captain says he does not think his boys have it, but agrees to search.
- The Tory looks closely around camp.
- The honey is hidden in a chest in a small cask.
- The captain opens the chest, puts his hand into the cask, pulls it out covered with honey, slams down the lid, wipes his hand, and says, "Boys what in thunder do you keep soap here for?"
- Daniel says, "We have to keep it there or the soldiers will steal it from us."
- A soldier's tongue is swollen from a bee sting.
- The old Tory asks what ails that man.
- The captain says, "He has the toothache."
- The old Tory goes away.

## Story 3: The Pork

Essential beats:

- Just before Lafayette came, the soldiers are almost starving.
- Daniel hears there is pork at home.
- He asks the captain to let him go get some.
- The captain says he cannot spare him.
- Daniel resolves to go anyway.
- He leaves that night.
- He is gone eight or ten days.
- He comes back carrying a back load of pork.
- He is taken for deserting.
- He is court-martialed and found guilty.
- He is asked why sentence of death should not be passed on him.
- Daniel says he has suffered enough to die a thousand deaths; if they want to kill him for what he has done, they may try, but he will fight his way through and escape or lose his life.
- The officers consult.
- Daniel is liberated.
- He divides the pork all around.
- The men have a good feast.

## Compression Notes For 24 Pages

The combined 24-page book cannot retell each anecdote at full 32-page-book scale. The clearest flow is:

- Pages 1-3: title, source note, opening frame.
- Pages 4-10: the night scouting story.
- Pages 11-19: hunger and the Tory honey story.
- Pages 20-23: the pork trip and court-martial.
- Page 24: quiet closing on what the stories remember.

The honey story gets the most middle space because it connects hunger, danger, wit, and the later pork story. The pork story should be direct, spare, and emotionally strong.
