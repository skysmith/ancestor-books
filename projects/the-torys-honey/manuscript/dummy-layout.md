# Captain Daniel and the Tory's Honey

## Format

- Standard 32-page picture book dummy
- Front matter assumption:
  - Page 1: half-title
  - Page 2: copyright
  - Page 3: title page
  - Page 4: blank or opening illustration
- Story begins on page 5

## Dummy Layout

### Spread 1 (Pages 5-6)

**Left Page**

The soldiers had taken the honey
in the night.

**Right Page**

But the story was not over.

The next morning
the old Tory came to the army camp.

**Illustration note:** Morning camp with a tense arrival, the Tory entering with purpose.

### Spread 2 (Pages 7-8)

**Left Page**

He wanted his honey back.

**Right Page**

The captain listened carefully.

"I do not think my boys have it," he said.

"But we will search."

### Spread 3 (Pages 9-10)

**Left Page**

So they walked
through the camp together.

**Right Page**

The Tory peered into every corner.

He looked into tents.

He examined boxes.

### Spread 4 (Pages 11-12)

**Left Page**

He searched
the soldiers' mess.

**Right Page**

At last he came
to a chest.

Inside was a small cask.

**Illustration note:** Suspense spread, the Tory's search narrowing toward the hidden honey.

### Spread 5 (Pages 13-14)

**Left Page**

The captain opened it.

**Right Page**

A soldier put his hand inside.

He pulled it out suddenly,
covered in honey.

### Spread 6 (Pages 15-16)

**Left Page**

Then he slammed down the lid.

**Right Page**

The soldier wiped his hand
with his handkerchief and said,

"Boys what in thunder
do you keep soap here for?"

**Illustration note:** Key comic-tension beat, honey on the hand, chest slammed shut, everyone trying to stay calm.

### Spread 7 (Pages 17-18)

**Left Page**

Daniel answered quickly.

"We have to keep it there
or the soldiers will steal it from us."

**Right Page**

The old Tory studied the men
carefully.

### Spread 8 (Pages 19-20)

**Left Page**

Just then he noticed
another soldier.

**Right Page**

The man stood
with his mouth wide open.

His tongue was swollen
from the sting of a bee.

### Spread 9 (Pages 21-22)

**Left Page**

The Tory pointed at him.

"What ails that man?"

**Right Page**

The captain replied calmly,

"He has the toothache."

**Illustration note:** Tight suspense moment, all eyes on the bee-stung soldier.

### Spread 10 (Pages 23-24)

**Left Page**

For a moment
the old Tory watched them.

**Right Page**

Then he nodded.

Satisfied,
he turned and left the camp.

### Spread 11 (Pages 25-26)

**Left Page**

The soldiers waited
until he was gone.

**Right Page**

Then they looked at one another.

And laughed.

### Spread 12 (Pages 27-28)

**Left Page**

Sometimes soldiers survived
by courage.

Sometimes they survived
by endurance.

**Right Page**

And sometimes,

a little quick thinking

was enough.

### Spread 13 (Pages 29-30)

**Full Spread**

Honey hidden in a chest--

**Illustration note:** Quiet symbolic spread, chest or cask in camp light, aftermath of a narrowly escaped discovery.

### Spread 14 (Pages 31-32)

**Full Spread**

and the laughter
that came
only after danger passed.

**Illustration note:** Very simple final image, soldiers sharing relieved laughter after the Tory has gone.

## Why This Layout Works

- The book starts in the middle of the problem, which keeps it distinct from Books 2-4.
- The camp search creates a tight suspense arc with several strong page turns.
- The preserved quotes land as the core dramatic beats.
- The ending releases tension through laughter rather than triumph.

## Possible Enhancement

If later revision wants a quieter close, Spread 12 could stop with "And laughed," leaving Spreads 13-14 to carry the emotional landing entirely through image.
