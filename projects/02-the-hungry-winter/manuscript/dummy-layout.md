# Captain Daniel and the Hungry Winter

## Format

- Standard 32-page picture book dummy
- Front matter assumption:
  - Page 1: half-title
  - Page 2: copyright
  - Page 3: title page
  - Page 4: blank or opening illustration
- Story begins on page 5

## Dummy Layout

### Spread 1 (Pages 5-6)

**Left Page**

Winter was hard on the soldiers.

The wind cut through their coats.

**Right Page**

The ground was cold.

The days were long.

And the food was almost gone.

**Illustration note:** Snowy or barren winter camp, soldiers hunched against wind, muted palette, immediate feeling of cold.

### Spread 2 (Pages 7-8)

**Left Page**

Daniel Cook and the men in his company
had marched and watched and waited
through hunger
that seemed to follow them everywhere.

**Right Page**

At first they had horse beef.

When that was gone,
they searched for anything else they could eat.

### Spread 3 (Pages 9-10)

**Left Page**

But no dogs
or any kind of meat
could be found.

**Right Page**

Still the hunger stayed.

It pressed and ached
and would not let go.

**Illustration note:** Sparse searching scene, men in bleak landscape, empty hands, no game, no relief.

### Spread 4 (Pages 11-12)

**Left Page**

At last the men grew so desperate
they roasted their shoes
and ate them.

**Right Page**

Even that was not enough.

**Illustration note:** Stark campfire scene with a boot or shoe near flame, soldiers gaunt and weary, handled with restraint rather than shock.

### Spread 5 (Pages 13-14)

**Left Page**

Faces grew hollow.

Steps grew slow.

Tempers grew short.

**Right Page**

The soldiers were hungry in the morning.

Hungry at noon.

Hungry again by night.

### Spread 6 (Pages 15-16)

**Left Page**

At last their captain spoke.

**Right Page**

"Go out and take some Tory's chickens," he said,
"or anything you can find."

**Illustration note:** Captain addressing exhausted men by low winter light; this quote should land clearly and simply.

### Spread 7 (Pages 17-18)

**Left Page**

The men knew of a Tory nearby.

He had food.

But he would sell none.

**Right Page**

So the soldiers made their plan.

They would go quietly.

They would go carefully.

They would go in the dark.

### Spread 8 (Pages 19-20)

**Left Page**

Daniel was chosen
to stand guard.

**Right Page**

The others moved ahead
through the night.

The air was sharp and still.

**Illustration note:** Split focus between Daniel holding watch and shadowy figures moving toward a distant Tory property.

### Spread 9 (Pages 21-22)

**Left Page**

Somewhere far off,
a branch cracked.

No one spoke.

No one laughed.

**Right Page**

Hunger had taken
all the extra words.

### Spread 10 (Pages 23-24)

**Left Page**

Daniel listened.

He watched.

He waited.

**Right Page**

The men were not looking for glory.

They were looking for supper.

**Illustration note:** Quiet guard scene, Daniel alert in darkness while the mission stays mostly off-page.

### Spread 11 (Pages 25-26)

**Left Page**

War could be that way.

One day brought musket fire and bullets.

**Right Page**

Another brought an empty stomach
and a long cold dark.

### Spread 12 (Pages 27-28)

**Left Page**

Still the soldiers went on.

Still they watched.

Still they obeyed.

**Right Page**

Because morning always came,

whether there was food enough
to meet it or not.

### Spread 13 (Pages 29-30)

**Full Spread**

And that night,
Daniel Cook went out into the dark
with the others--

**Illustration note:** Wide night scene, Daniel and the soldiers moving forward into darkness, small against winter land.

### Spread 14 (Pages 31-32)

**Full Spread**

hoping they might find enough
to keep marching
one more day.

**Illustration note:** Very simple final image, soldiers disappearing into the dark or faint dawn, emphasizing endurance rather than triumph.

## Why This Layout Works

- The early spreads dwell on physical hunger before the plot turns outward.
- The captain's order lands as the emotional pivot at mid-book.
- Daniel's role as guard gives the second half a quiet tension without stepping into Book 3's honey incident.
- The ending stays reflective and restrained, matching Book 1's emotional approach.

## Possible Enhancement

If later revisions need a sharper action arc, spreads 8-10 could be expanded to include the approach to the Tory house and the first sign of the dog, leaving the honey trick for Book 3.
