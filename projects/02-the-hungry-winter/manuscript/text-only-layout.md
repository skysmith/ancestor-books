### Spread 1 (Pages 5-6)

Left:
Winter was hard on the soldiers.

The wind cut through their coats.

Right:
The ground was cold.

The days were long.

And the food was almost gone.

### Spread 2 (Pages 7-8)

Left:
Daniel Cook and the men in his company
had marched and watched and waited
through hunger
that seemed to follow them everywhere.

Right:
At first they had horse beef.

When that was gone,
they searched for anything else they could eat.

### Spread 3 (Pages 9-10)

Left:
But no dogs
or any kind of meat
could be found.

Right:
Still the hunger stayed.

It pressed and ached
and would not let go.

### Spread 4 (Pages 11-12)

Left:
At last the men grew so desperate
they roasted their shoes
and ate them.

Right:
Even that was not enough.

### Spread 5 (Pages 13-14)

Left:
Faces grew hollow.

Steps grew slow.

Tempers grew short.

Right:
The soldiers were hungry in the morning.

Hungry at noon.

Hungry again by night.

### Spread 6 (Pages 15-16)

Left:
At last their captain spoke.

Right:
"Go out and take some Tory's chickens," he said,
"or anything you can find."

### Spread 7 (Pages 17-18)

Left:
The men knew of a Tory nearby.

He had food.

But he would sell none.

Right:
So the soldiers made their plan.

They would go quietly.

They would go carefully.

They would go in the dark.

### Spread 8 (Pages 19-20)

Left:
Daniel was chosen
to stand guard.

Right:
The others moved ahead
through the night.

The air was sharp and still.

### Spread 9 (Pages 21-22)

Left:
Somewhere far off,
a branch cracked.

No one spoke.

No one laughed.

Right:
Hunger had taken
all the extra words.

### Spread 10 (Pages 23-24)

Left:
Daniel listened.

He watched.

He waited.

Right:
The men were not looking for glory.

They were looking for supper.

### Spread 11 (Pages 25-26)

Left:
War could be that way.

One day brought musket fire and bullets.

Right:
Another brought an empty stomach
and a long cold dark.

### Spread 12 (Pages 27-28)

Left:
Still the soldiers went on.

Still they watched.

Still they obeyed.

Right:
Because morning always came,

whether there was food enough
to meet it or not.

### Spread 13 (Pages 29-30)

And that night,
Daniel Cook went out into the dark
with the others--

### Spread 14 (Pages 31-32)

hoping they might find enough
to keep marching
one more day.
