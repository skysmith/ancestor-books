# Storyboard Production Checklist

## Goal

Move Book 2 from dummy layout into a reviewable rough visual dummy.

## Start Point

Required inputs:

- `manuscript/dummy-layout.md`
- `art/briefs/illustration-brief.md`
- `art/briefs/thumbnail-sheet.md`

## Anchor Spreads To Build First

1. Spread 1: cold opening camp
2. Spread 4: roasting shoes
3. Spread 6: captain's order
4. Spread 8: Daniel chosen to stand guard
5. Spread 10: Daniel listening in the dark
6. Spread 14: final endurance image

## Phase 1: Thumbnail Pass

- Draw all front matter pages plus all 14 story spreads
- Solve only silhouette, value, and text-safe zones
- Keep weather and wear consistent
- Mark where warmth appears and make it scarce

## Phase 2: Rough Image Comps

- Generate rough comps for the six anchor spreads first
- Match painterly historical realism
- Keep lighting logic consistent: cold daylight, dim firelight, then dark mission night
- Avoid overstating facial suffering; use posture and spacing

## Phase 3: Full Storyboard Assembly

- Build a full-board sequence in spread order
- Check that the emotional center stays with hardship rather than action
- Keep the mission section quiet
- Confirm the ending feels small and true

## Phase 4: Revision Pass

- Reduce any spread that becomes too dramatic or heroic
- Make hunger legible through repetition and environment
- Simplify text collisions in spreads with group scenes
- Keep Daniel young and consistent with the rest of the series

## Practical Output Targets

- Full contact sheet
- Six stronger anchor roughs
- Review PDF with text integrated

## Done Criteria

- The book feels colder than the others
- The shoe-roasting spread is powerful but restrained
- The captain's quote lands clearly
- The final spread ends in persistence, not victory
