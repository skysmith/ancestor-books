# Illustration Brief

## Project

**Title:** Captain Daniel and the Hungry Winter  
**Format:** 32-page picture book dummy  
**Audience:** Picture-book readers; historically grounded, solemn, and emotionally clear  
**Story function:** A winter hardship story about hunger, endurance, and moving forward without relief

## Visual Goal

Make this book feel colder, quieter, and more physically draining than the others. The tension does not come from one sudden attack. It comes from the long, grinding pressure of need.

The emotional center is not triumph. It is endurance. The art should let children feel the weight of cold, empty stomachs, and soldiers continuing because they must.

## Overall Art Direction

- Use painterly historical realism with restrained emotion.
- Favor atmosphere, posture, and repetition over spectacle.
- Let hunger show through body language, pace, spacing, and worn surfaces.
- Keep scenes simple and readable; this story benefits from visual austerity.
- Avoid graphic treatment of deprivation. The emotional effect should come from contrast and restraint.

## Tone And Pacing

- Early spreads: cold, emptiness, attrition
- Middle spreads: desperation and command
- Late spreads: quiet mission tension
- Final spreads: reflective endurance

## Character Notes

### Daniel Cook

- Young soldier, still recognizably the same Daniel as the other books
- Less active here than in Book 1; he is part of a suffering company first, individual second
- His role becomes more focused in the second half when he stands guard

### Captain

- Worn but decisive
- Gives the critical order without theatrics
- Should feel like a man burdened by necessity

### Other Soldiers

- Gaunt, tired, and slow-moving
- Distinct enough to feel like a company, but not individually spotlighted for long
- Their shared suffering is the visual field of the book

## Setting Notes

- Winter camp and bleak surrounding land
- Limited food, limited movement, limited comfort
- A Tory property exists later in the story, but the camp is the dominant environment
- The second half should move toward darkness and quiet rather than opening into action

## Palette And Light

- Dominant palette: gray-blue cold, dirty snow, brown wool, smoke-charcoal, muted earth
- Accent lights: dim firelight, faint dawn, very limited warmth
- Warmth should always feel scarce
- Final spreads can remain subdued rather than resolving into bright relief

## Recurring Visual Motifs

- Wind and cold
- Smoke and ash
- Empty hands / little food
- Boots and worn clothing
- Small groups bent together against weather

## Continuity Priorities

- Daniel should stay consistent with the earlier books
- Winter hardship should increase gradually, not jump randomly spread to spread
- Shoes, coats, and camp gear should visibly wear down
- The Tory-house mission should feel like an outgrowth of hunger, not a separate adventure

## Front Matter Suggestions

### Page 1: Half-Title

- Snow, a worn shoe, or a cold campfire ember

### Page 2: Copyright

- Minimal spot art or open page

### Page 3: Title Page

- Daniel in winter camp before the story fully opens

### Page 4: Opening Illustration / Silent Lead-In

- Empty cold camp or barren ground before text begins

## Spread-By-Spread Art Notes

### Spread 1 (Pages 5-6)

**Text beat:** Winter was hard; cold surrounds the soldiers.

**Visual objective:** Establish physical discomfort immediately.

**Composition ideas:**

- Soldiers huddled or bent in wind
- Cold landscape larger than the men

**Important details:**

- The camp should feel exposed

### Spread 2 (Pages 7-8)

**Text beat:** Hunger has followed the company through marching and waiting.

**Visual objective:** Make hunger feel constant rather than dramatic.

**Composition ideas:**

- Small routines of survival
- Men waiting, moving slowly, carrying little

**Important details:**

- Repetition helps this spread feel true

### Spread 3 (Pages 9-10)

**Text beat:** No other food can be found; hunger stays.

**Visual objective:** Show the bleak absence of options.

**Composition ideas:**

- Searching, barren land, empty hands
- Daniel and others visually dwarfed by emptiness

**Important details:**

- Lack should be visible everywhere

### Spread 4 (Pages 11-12)

**Text beat:** The men roast their shoes and even that is not enough.

**Visual objective:** Reach the story's most severe hardship image.

**Composition ideas:**

- Campfire with shoe near flame
- Faces not shocked, just exhausted

**Important details:**

- Handle this with dignity, not sensationalism

### Spread 5 (Pages 13-14)

**Text beat:** Faces hollow, steps slow, tempers short; hunger all day long.

**Visual objective:** Show the cumulative effect on the company.

**Composition ideas:**

- Sequence-like composition or grouped figures in different states of fatigue

**Important details:**

- This spread should feel like duration

### Spread 6 (Pages 15-16)

**Text beat:** The captain gives the order to find chickens or anything else.

**Visual objective:** Mark the story's turning point.

**Composition ideas:**

- Captain framed by weary men
- Low light and emotional seriousness

**Important details:**

- The preserved quote should land plainly and with weight

### Spread 7 (Pages 17-18)

**Text beat:** The soldiers know of a Tory nearby and make a careful plan.

**Visual objective:** Shift from suffering to intention.

**Composition ideas:**

- Group planning scene with dark horizon or distant property

**Important details:**

- Keep this tense, not exciting

### Spread 8 (Pages 19-20)

**Text beat:** Daniel is chosen to stand guard while others move ahead.

**Visual objective:** Narrow the story toward Daniel's role.

**Composition ideas:**

- Split scene between Daniel and the moving men

**Important details:**

- The second half should feel quieter, not bigger

### Spread 9 (Pages 21-22)

**Text beat:** The branch cracks; no one speaks; hunger has taken extra words.

**Visual objective:** Show silence as a product of hardship.

**Composition ideas:**

- Dark woods or edge of property
- Small sounds in a large silence

**Important details:**

- Very little motion needed here

### Spread 10 (Pages 23-24)

**Text beat:** Daniel listens, watches, and waits; the men are searching for supper, not glory.

**Visual objective:** Center the mission's humility.

**Composition ideas:**

- Daniel alone, alert in the dark
- Others only implied or distant

**Important details:**

- This is quiet tension, not action

### Spread 11 (Pages 25-26)

**Text beat:** War brings bullets one day and hunger the next.

**Visual objective:** Frame hunger as part of war, not outside it.

**Composition ideas:**

- Reflective spread with visual contrast between soldier and winter landscape

**Important details:**

- Avoid illustration that becomes too symbolic too early

### Spread 12 (Pages 27-28)

**Text beat:** Still they went on; morning always came whether there was food or not.

**Visual objective:** Carry endurance toward its emotional conclusion.

**Composition ideas:**

- Dawn or dim horizon
- Men continuing despite exhaustion

**Important details:**

- This spread should feel humble and true

### Spread 13 (Pages 29-30)

**Text beat:** Daniel and the others go out into the dark.

**Visual objective:** Move from hardship into a final mission image.

**Composition ideas:**

- Wide night journey image
- Small figures crossing winter land

**Important details:**

- Keep them vulnerable in scale

### Spread 14 (Pages 31-32)

**Text beat:** They hope only for enough to keep marching one more day.

**Visual objective:** End on persistence, not victory.

**Composition ideas:**

- Minimal final image with distance and cold air

**Important details:**

- Let silence do part of the work

## Research Checklist

- Period winter camp gear and fire setup
- Revolutionary War clothing in worn winter condition
- Footwear and plausible deterioration
- Tory farmhouse distance / layout appropriate to New England
- Firelight and smoke behavior in outdoor camp settings

## Open Art Decisions

- How much snow versus bare frozen earth the setting should show
- Whether the shoe-roasting image should be direct or partly implied
- Whether the final spread leans more nightward or dawnward

## Deliverable Recommendation

After this brief, create a thumbnail sheet emphasizing:

- the cold opening
- the shoe-roasting spread
- the captain's order
- Daniel standing guard
- the final small figures moving into the dark
