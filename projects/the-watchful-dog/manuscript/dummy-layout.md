# Captain Daniel and the Watchful Dog

## Format

- Standard 32-page picture book dummy
- Front matter assumption:
  - Page 1: half-title
  - Page 2: copyright
  - Page 3: title page
  - Page 4: blank or opening illustration
- Story begins on page 5

## Dummy Layout

### Spread 1 (Pages 5-6)

**Left Page**

The soldiers were still hungry.

Winter had stretched on for weeks.

**Right Page**

Food was scarce in the camp.

At last the captain gave the order.

**Illustration note:** Winter camp with hunger already established, soldiers tired and cold.

### Spread 2 (Pages 7-8)

**Left Page**

"Go out and take some Tory's chickens," he said,
"or anything you can find."

**Right Page**

The soldiers knew of a man nearby who had food.

But the Tory guarded his house carefully.

### Spread 3 (Pages 9-10)

**Left Page**

He kept a large bulldog
to watch the yard.

**Right Page**

No one could come near in the night
without waking the beast.

**Illustration note:** Tory house in darkness, the bulldog a looming presence near the yard.

### Spread 4 (Pages 11-12)

**Left Page**

So the soldiers made a plan.

Daniel Cook would stand guard.

**Right Page**

The others would slip quietly
toward the bee house and the barns
to see what food they might find.

### Spread 5 (Pages 13-14)

**Left Page**

The night was dark.

The air was cold.

Daniel walked slowly toward the house.

**Right Page**

For a moment
everything was still.

### Spread 6 (Pages 15-16)

**Left Page**

Then the dog came.

It rushed from the darkness
in a great fury.

**Right Page**

Daniel saw at once
that he must defend himself.

**Illustration note:** Major action spread: bulldog bursting out of the darkness toward Daniel.

### Spread 7 (Pages 17-18)

**Left Page**

He raised his musket
and fired.

The dog fell.

**Right Page**

The shot brought the Tory
to the door in a rage.

### Spread 8 (Pages 19-20)

**Left Page**

He shouted to know
why the soldier had fired.

**Right Page**

Daniel answered calmly.

**Illustration note:** Daniel in cold night light, steady while the angry Tory leans from the doorway.

### Spread 9 (Pages 21-22)

**Left Page**

He told the man
he had shot the dog
because every time he stood guard
he was in danger
of being torn to pieces.

**Right Page**

The old Tory scolded
and complained.

### Spread 10 (Pages 23-24)

**Left Page**

Daniel listened.

He stood in the cold
and spoke with him
for some time.

**Right Page**

At last the Tory turned
and went back inside.

### Spread 11 (Pages 25-26)

**Left Page**

When Daniel returned to camp
he discovered something.

**Right Page**

While the Tory had been arguing with him
outside in the cold...

**Illustration note:** Transition spread, Daniel returning through darkness while the reader anticipates the reveal.

### Spread 12 (Pages 27-28)

**Left Page**

the other soldiers
had slipped into the bee house.

**Right Page**

And they had taken honey.

### Spread 13 (Pages 29-30)

**Full Spread**

Daniel had stood in the dark
and held the old man there--

**Illustration note:** Wide reflective spread, Daniel small against the night, Tory at the doorway, bee house or camp implied in the distance.

### Spread 14 (Pages 31-32)

**Full Spread**

long enough
for the others
to come away with sweetness
in a hungry winter.

**Illustration note:** Final quiet image, perhaps soldiers carrying the stolen honey back through the dark or a simple honey-glow in darkness.

## Why This Layout Works

- The opening moves quickly from hunger to the guarded house.
- The bulldog charge and musket shot get the visual emphasis they need.
- Daniel's calm exchange with the Tory becomes the emotional center.
- The ending lands on the reveal that his steadiness made the honey theft possible.

## Possible Enhancement

If later revision needs an even quieter ending, Spread 14 could end simply on the words "And they had taken honey," with the final spread carrying only image.
