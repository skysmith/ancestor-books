# Series Visual Direction

## Current Correction

The first image passes proved two things:

- the painterly historical direction is working
- night scenes can become too dim and too compositionally repetitive

This series should stay atmospheric without becoming muddy.

## Night Scene Rule

- Night scenes must remain readable at a glance.
- Use moonlight, snow bounce, lantern glow, doorway light, dawn edge light, or reflected ambient light to keep forms visible.
- Avoid near-black scenes where figures disappear.
- Keep contrast controlled, but never so low that story action becomes hard to read.

## Diversity Rule

Each book should vary the visual job of its spreads.

Across a sequence, deliberately mix:

- wide environment-led spreads
- object-led close-ups
- medium group tableaux
- distance silhouettes
- prop-focused suspense images
- quiet minimal endings

Avoid repeating:

- Daniel in foreground on every spread
- same blue/orange palette balance on every spread
- same medium-close camera distance
- same expression of worry on every face

## Emotional Readability

- Faces should be clear enough for children to read quickly.
- Tension should come from silence, light, gesture, and spacing as much as facial expression.
- Relief scenes should feel lighter, not louder.

## Historical Tone

- Painterly realism stays the base language.
- No glossy fantasy lighting.
- No action-movie exaggeration.
- No graphic violence.
